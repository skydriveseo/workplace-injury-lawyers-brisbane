  • Promote safety education and awareness

The majority of safety experts will agree that adequate training is just as critical as policy, if not more important. Employees must comprehend the hazards present at work, as well as how and why the tasks they perform might result in damage or even death. It's critical to comprehend the factors that contribute to injuries, both the dangers involved and the nature of the wounds themselves. These dangers are multifaceted and not the result of one specific thing. 

  • Provide them with PPE kits

Employers might well be required to offer some kind of PPE to employees, depending on province rules. Protective equipment, such as gloves as well as face masks, is unquestionably required in a variety of industries during the present epidemic. Providing sufficient PPE is another important factor in boosting worker trust.

  • Make it a rule to be in proper uniform

Wearing the appropriate protective clothes is necessary in addition to PPE. Additionally, it's not only about the clothing; it's also about the fit. A major danger is loose or poorly fitted garments that can be snagged or stumbled over (particularly for the women, who feel uncomfortable in the ill-fitting PPE). 

  • Keep your workplace tidy as well as communicate efficiently

Keeping a workspace or site tidy and clean is a basic that certain businesses seem to overlook. Maintain inventory, ensure the floor is clear and entrances are not obstructed, offer safety information sheets for chemicals on-site, and so on. All of these are easy tasks that must be completed. Additionally, signs should be utilized to keep personnel informed.

  • Check out for safety hazards

Finally, while it may appear to be a simple step, identifying which dangers are present in your workplace is an essential aspect of accident prevention. The very same rules can't be followed uniformly. In addition, increasing inspections of locations and equipment is a wonderful method to keep watch on possible hazards as well as possibilities for improvement.
